ABOUT

About

I like building useful things, especially the kind that make messy work feel a little calmer and more manageable. Most of my background is in technical project work, research-heavy digital projects, and the occasional side tool that scratches a very specific itch. I’ve spent years moving between planning, people, and systems, which usually means turning vague ideas into something usable. Lately that’s also meant building small browser tools and local-first projects, because I like things that are practical, clean, and don’t try too hard.

The work shown here leans toward practical software: browser extensions, local-first organization tools, and data-focused utilities built to solve narrow real-world problems.
